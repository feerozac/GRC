PROBLEM SPACE ANALYSIS

Governance is broken
by design.

Organisations have built GRC around documents, silos, and periodic reviews. Then they added AI. Now they have faster outputs — with less trust, less traceability, and more questions than answers. This is the problem Evonix exists to solve.

02 — Soft Systems View

Six people. Six realities.

In soft systems terms, the GRC problem is not one problem — it is six competing worldviews, each with its own definition of success, its own frustrations, and its own blind spots. No tool can work until it speaks all six languages.

🎯
The CISO
2nd Line — Risk Posture Owner
"I need to tell the board we're secure. But I can't see across 1L, 2L, and 3L in one place. My board pack takes two weeks to assemble."
Pain: fragmented reporting, no unified risk posture view
📋
The Compliance Manager
2nd Line — Framework & Regulatory
"We map to ISO, NIST, COBIT, HKMA, and MAS. Separately. In different spreadsheets. Every framework update is a fire drill."
Pain: manual multi-framework mapping, regulatory lag
🔍
The Internal Auditor
3rd Line — Independent Assurance
"I ask for evidence and wait days. When it arrives, I can't trace it back to the control, let alone the risk. And now they want me to audit AI outputs I can't explain."
Pain: evidence chasing, no AI explainability for assurance
🛡️
The SOC Lead
1st/2nd Line — Cyber Operations
"My alerts are in Splunk. Their risks are in a spreadsheet. An incident happens and governance doesn't know for a week. We close incidents but nobody updates the risk register."
Pain: cyber-GRC disconnect, no closed loop
👔
The Board Member
Governance — Oversight & Fiduciary Duty
"I get a 60-page deck once a quarter. I don't know which AI made which recommendation or whether anyone challenged it. I'm told to trust the process. I can't see the process."
Pain: opacity, no line-of-sight from strategy to control
⚖️
The Regulator
External — Supervisory Authority
"Show me how this AI decision was made. Show me who reviewed it. Show me the challenge history. Show me the audit trail. You have two minutes."
Pain: no regulator-ready evidence, no decision traceability
03 — Systemic Tensions

The five pulls that break GRC.

These are not problems to solve — they are tensions to hold. Every GRC system fails when it collapses one pole into the other. The art is in the architecture that holds both.

Speed

Faster decisions, automated workflows, real-time response. The board wants answers now.

vs

Assurance

Every decision auditable, every output explainable, every action traceable. The regulator wants proof.

Agent Autonomy

AI agents that act continuously, triage alerts, draft policies, recommend controls at machine speed.

vs

Human Accountability

Someone must own the decision. A human must be able to challenge, override, and explain.

Framework Breadth

ISO, NIST, COBIT, HKMA, MAS, PCI DSS, Basel — every buyer has a different combination.

vs

Control Depth

Mapping to a framework is easy. Proving that mappings are accurate, current, and evidence-backed is hard.

Cyber Operations

SOC teams live in real-time. Alerts, incidents, response actions — measured in seconds.

vs

Governance Cadence

GRC teams live in quarters. Reviews, reports, attestations — measured in weeks.

Innovation Pressure

Deploy AI everywhere. Move fast. Automate everything. Competitive advantage demands it.

vs

Regulatory Gravity

EU AI Act. HKMA GenAI. MAS TRM. ISO 42001. The compliance surface area is expanding, not shrinking.

05 — Why Now

Four forces converging.

The GRC problem is not new. What's new is that four forces are converging simultaneously, creating both existential risk for organisations that don't adapt — and a once-in-a-decade opening for a platform that gets it right.

🤖

The Agentic AI Moment

AI agents are moving from chat assistants to autonomous actors — triaging alerts, drafting policies, recommending controls. But who governs the governors? Agent identity, delegation, and per-action authorization are unsolved in GRC.

📜

The Regulatory Acceleration

EU AI Act mandates explainability. HKMA GenAI circular requires documentation. ISO 42001 formalises AI management systems. MAS TRM tightens technology risk. The compliance surface area is growing faster than teams can map it.

🔗

The Cyber-GRC Convergence

Boards now hold CISOs accountable for both cyber defence and governance reporting. SOC teams and risk teams can no longer operate on different timescales. Incidents must feed risk posture and control evidence — in real time, not next quarter.

🏛️

The Trust Deficit

AI is generating more outputs faster. But trust is declining. Boards want to see how decisions were made. Regulators want to see who challenged them. Auditors want to replay them. No incumbent GRC platform provides per-decision evidence with structured challenge and audit trail.

06 — The Thesis

The trust layer for agentic GRC.

Evonix is not another GRC dashboard. It is the trust architecture that makes agentic governance possible — where every recommendation is explainable, every decision is challengeable, and every action is auditable.

PILLAR 01
Explainability Evidence Fabric
Every AI output produces a DecisionLog — sources cited, confidence scored, reasoning chained, framework mapped. Structured challenge mechanism. Regulator-ready report in one click.
KPI: Regulator packet in < 2 minutes
PILLAR 02
Controlled Agent Autonomy
Agents as coworkers, not plugins. Own identity, scoped delegation, per-action authorization. Fail-closed operational states. No agent can escalate its own permissions.
KPI: Per-action authorization < 200ms p99
PILLAR 03
Regulatory Change-to-Action
HKMA and MAS updates ingested within 24 hours. Normalised, mapped to controls, impact-assessed with AI confidence scoring. Gap becomes backlog in one workflow.
KPI: 95% of updates within 24h SLA
PILLAR 04
Cyber-to-GRC Closed Loop
SIEM alerts flow through Detection, Response, and Orchestration agents. Every incident links to risks and controls before closure. Cyber events feed governance posture by default.
KPI: 90% critical incidents linked before closure

The problem is not that organisations lack GRC tools. It is that their tools were built for a world where governance was periodic, manual, and siloed. That world is gone.

In the agentic era, governance must be continuous, explainable, and trustworthy. The platform that delivers this becomes the trust layer for every AI-powered organisation.

That platform is Evonix.
