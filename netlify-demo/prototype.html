Offering & Roadmap

Overview

The Evonix platform transforms boardroom priorities into actionable control objectives. Using AI-powered agents and explainable decision-making, organizations maintain continuous compliance while reducing manual audit preparation time.

The platform bridges the gap between strategic governance decisions and operational control implementation through automated mapping, intelligent recommendations, and real-time monitoring.

Key Capabilities

  • Boardroom Theme Extraction
    AI-powered extraction of strategic priorities from board documents
  • Framework Mapping
    Automatic alignment to COBIT, COSO, ISO 27001, NIST CSF, and regulatory frameworks
  • Control Tower
    Real-time monitoring of KCIs, KRIs, and risk appetite alignment
  • Explainable AI
    Full transparency into AI decision rationale with source citations
  • Regulatory Risk Strategy
    Regulation-to-artifact mapping with evidence produced by default operation
  • Org + Document Intelligence
    Org chart mapping and annual report/strategy analysis into governance issues

Three Lines of Defense Model

The platform implements a comprehensive three-line defense model through specialized AI agents:

1st Line: Operations Agent

Control Implementation & Evidence
  • Defines and implements operational controls
  • Manages evidence collection and documentation
  • Ensures day-to-day compliance with policies
  • Performs control self-assessments (RCSA)

2nd Line: Risk Agent

Risk Oversight & Governance
  • Monitors risk posture and appetite alignment
  • Maps controls to governance frameworks
  • Reviews and challenges 1st line assessments
  • Manages regulatory change impact analysis

3rd Line: Audit Agent

Independent Assurance
  • Provides independent control testing
  • Validates evidence quality and completeness
  • Identifies control gaps and weaknesses
  • Supports external audit preparation

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I upload boardroom documents?

Navigate to the Workflow page and use the "Upload Document" button. Supported formats include PDF, DOCX, and plain text. You can also paste content directly.

What governance frameworks are supported?

The platform supports COBIT 2019, COSO ERM, ISO 27001, NIST CSF, HKMA, MAS TRM, SOC 2, PCI DSS, GDPR, and Basel III. Custom frameworks can be added.

How does the AI extraction work?

The AI analyzes boardroom documents to identify strategic themes, risk statements, and compliance commitments, then maps these to governance objectives with full explainability.

What is the three-line defense model?

The three-line model separates responsibilities: 1st Line (Operations) manages controls, 2nd Line (Risk) provides oversight, and 3rd Line (Audit) delivers independent assurance.

How do I challenge an AI decision?

In the Explainability Panel, click "Challenge" to flag a decision for review. Provide your rationale and it will be routed to the appropriate stakeholder.

AI Security & IAM for Agents

Aligned
Design priority: Security > Stability > Performance > Intelligence. Agents operate as coworkers with separate identity, explicit delegation, and real-time authorization—not ahead-of-time OAuth scopes.

North Star

1. Security — Minimize harm, blast radius, irreversibility
2. Stability — Predictability, debuggability, failure containment
3. Performance — Reduce friction and wasted effort
4. Intelligence — Usefulness within the above constraints

When tradeoffs arise, the higher-priority constraint wins.

Coworker Model

  • Identity — Agent has its own accounts; never uses human credentials
  • Authority — Explicitly delegated, revocable; no shared sessions
  • Execution — Constrained by infrastructure, tooling, review

Separation of identity, authority, and execution. Compromise in one does not propagate.

IAM for Agents — OAuth vs Real-Time Authorization

❌ Ahead of time (OAuth-style)

  • Predefined scopes (e.g. calendar.read, calendar.write)
  • Known, static clients; consent baked in before the call
  • API only checks "was approval granted?"

Breaks with AI agents: intent and behavior are not known ahead of time; tool/API calls are composable; behavior emerges at runtime. Authorizing ahead of time is dangerous.

✓ Real-time, context-aware

Authorization for AI agents must become:

Is this action acceptable, at this specific moment, given what's already happened, which derives from some authority?

Evonix: every agent action has rationale, confidence, and audit trail; material actions require human approval (HITL).
