# Terms of Reference (TOR) — Tech Risk Forum

> Governance framework for identifying, assessing, deciding, and tracking technology risk across Evonix.

**Document owner:** Chief Risk Officer (or delegate)  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** [Insert date]  
**Review cycle:** Quarterly (minimum), full annual refresh

---

## 1) Purpose

The Tech Risk Forum is a cross-functional governance body that provides oversight of technology risk, ensures material risks are managed within risk appetite, and supports informed decision-making by leadership.

The forum is intended to:

- Improve visibility of technology risk across business and engineering functions.
- Make timely, accountable risk decisions (accept, mitigate, transfer, avoid).
- Track remediation progress for high and critical risks.
- Escalate material issues to executive governance forums.
- Strengthen evidence for audit, regulatory, and board reporting.

---

## 2) Objectives

The Tech Risk Forum will:

1. Review and challenge the technology risk profile and trend movements.
2. Ensure material risks have named owners, treatment plans, target dates, and status updates.
3. Monitor key risk indicators (KRIs), key control indicators (KCIs), incidents, vulnerabilities, and exceptions.
4. Oversee risks relating to cyber, cloud, AI/ML, data, resiliency, third parties, and engineering delivery.
5. Confirm that accepted risks are explicitly documented with rationale, expiry, and approval.
6. Recommend policy, control, or investment changes where risk exposure is outside appetite.

---

## 3) Scope

### In scope

- Technology and cyber risk themes, including:
  - Security architecture and vulnerabilities.
  - Cloud/platform risks and operational resilience.
  - AI/ML and model governance risks (e.g., explainability, oversight, misuse).
  - Data governance and privacy-related technical controls.
  - Third-party technology and concentration risks.
  - Material delivery risks impacting control effectiveness.
- Risk exceptions and risk acceptance decisions within delegated authority.
- Progress and effectiveness of remediation actions.

### Out of scope

- Day-to-day technical operations and incident command (handled by operational teams).
- Individual project steering decisions unless they present material technology risk.
- Matters outside delegated authority requiring direct executive or board approval.

---

## 4) Authority and decision rights

The forum is authorized to:

- Approve or reject risk treatment plans within delegated thresholds.
- Approve risk acceptances within delegated risk appetite limits.
- Require remedial actions, deadlines, and accountable owners.
- Escalate breaches, unresolved high-risk items, or systemic control weaknesses.

The forum is **not** authorized to:

- Override legal/regulatory obligations.
- Approve risk acceptance above delegated tolerance limits.
- Replace accountable executive ownership for risks.

---

## 5) Membership

### Core members (voting)

- Chair: Chief Risk Officer (or Head of Technology Risk)
- Technology/CISO representative
- Engineering leadership representative
- Product/Platform leadership representative
- Data/Privacy representative
- Compliance representative
- Internal Controls / 2nd line representative

### Standing attendees (non-voting unless delegated)

- Internal Audit observer (as appropriate)
- Program Management Office (PMO)
- Finance/Procurement (for investment or third-party topics)
- Secretariat (agenda, minutes, actions, reporting)

### Quorum

A quorum is met when:

- Chair (or approved delegate) is present, **and**
- At least 50% of core voting members are present, including one 1st line and one 2nd line representative.

---

## 6) Roles and responsibilities

### Chair

- Sets agenda priorities and ensures balanced challenge.
- Confirms decisions, owners, and deadlines.
- Escalates unresolved or out-of-tolerance issues.

### Risk owners (1st line)

- Present current risk posture, incident learnings, and treatment progress.
- Maintain risk records with clear impact, likelihood, controls, and action plans.
- Deliver agreed remediation actions on time.

### 2nd line Risk/Compliance

- Provide independent challenge and appetite interpretation.
- Verify consistency of risk rating and acceptance rationale.
- Support quality of KRI/KCI and governance evidence.

### Secretariat

- Coordinate agenda, papers, and pre-reads.
- Record decisions, action items, due dates, and owners.
- Publish minutes and action tracker within agreed SLA.

---

## 7) Meeting cadence and format

- **Frequency:** Monthly (minimum).  
- **Duration:** 60–90 minutes.  
- **Extraordinary sessions:** May be convened by the Chair for urgent issues.

### Standard agenda (recommended)

1. Approval of prior minutes and action status.
2. Technology risk dashboard (KRIs/KCIs, trend and heat map).
3. Top risks deep dive (high/critical).
4. Incident and near-miss lessons learned.
5. Risk acceptances/exceptions for decision.
6. Remediation status and overdue actions.
7. Escalations and decisions required.
8. AOB and next meeting priorities.

---

## 8) Inputs and required artefacts

Each meeting pack should include:

- Updated top risk register (with movement from prior month).
- KRI/KCI dashboard with thresholds and breaches.
- Open action log with RAG status and due dates.
- Summary of new incidents, vulnerabilities, and control failures.
- Papers for risk acceptances/exceptions requiring decision.

All decisions must be traceable to documented evidence.

---

## 9) Outputs and deliverables

The forum will produce:

- Approved meeting minutes (including decisions and rationale).
- Action tracker with accountable owner and delivery dates.
- Escalation papers for executive risk forum (where required).
- Monthly summary report of risk posture and significant changes.
- Quarterly trend report and thematic analysis.

---

## 10) Escalation criteria

Automatic escalation to Executive Risk Committee (or equivalent) occurs when:

- Risk appetite is breached or likely to be breached.
- A high/critical risk remains untreated past agreed tolerance.
- A systemic control weakness impacts multiple domains.
- A regulatory or customer-impacting issue is identified.
- Required remediation is materially off-track without credible recovery.

---

## 11) Performance and effectiveness

Forum effectiveness will be measured using:

- % of high/critical risks with approved treatment plans.
- % of remediation actions completed by target date.
- Number and age profile of overdue high-risk actions.
- Time to decision for risk acceptances/exceptions.
- Reduction in repeat incidents/control failures.

An annual self-assessment will confirm whether the forum remains effective, appropriately scoped, and aligned with enterprise governance.

---

## 12) Documentation and records retention

- Minutes, decisions, and action logs are retained in the approved governance repository.
- Risk acceptance records must include expiry date, rationale, and approving authority.
- Artefacts should be audit-ready and accessible for regulatory review.

---

## 13) Review and amendment

This TOR must be reviewed:

- At least annually, and
- Earlier if major changes occur in regulation, operating model, risk appetite, or organizational structure.

Changes require Chair approval and ratification by the relevant executive governance forum where applicable.

## Appendix A — Decision matrix (example)

| Decision type | Tech Risk Forum | Executive Risk Committee |
|---|---|---|
| Approve treatment plan (within threshold) | Approve | Inform |
| Approve risk acceptance (within delegated authority) | Approve | Inform |
| Approve risk acceptance (above delegated authority) | Recommend | Approve |
| Appetite breach / likely breach | Escalate | Decide |
| Systemic control weakness | Escalate with recommendation | Decide |

---

## Appendix B — Minimum paper template for risk acceptance

1. Risk statement and category  
2. Inherent and residual rating  
3. Controls in place and control gaps  
4. Treatment options considered  
5. Rationale for acceptance  
6. Compensating controls  
7. Expiry/review date  
8. Owner, approver, and impacted stakeholders
