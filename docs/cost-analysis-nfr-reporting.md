## Executive Summary

Global financial institutions spend **$206–$270 billion annually** on compliance, with **73% still relying on manual processes**. Non-financial risk (NFR) reporting — the manual reading, extraction, categorisation, and reporting of risk data from documents — is one of the most labour-intensive, least automated functions in banking. We estimate the NFR document processing cost at **$6–$15M per year per G-SIB**, supported by the bottom-up analysis below.

## 3. Bottom-Up Cost Model: NFR Document Processing

### The Manual Process (What Happens Today)

At a Top 10 bank, the NFR reporting cycle involves:

1. **Document Collection** — Gather regulatory filings, committee papers, board circulars, incident reports, policy documents (~500–2,000 documents per quarter)
2. **Document Reading** — Analysts read each document, identify risk events, control failures, regulatory exposures
3. **Data Extraction** — Manually extract and type findings into spreadsheets or risk register
4. **Taxonomy Categorisation** — Map each finding to the bank's risk taxonomy (Basel categories, internal categories)
5. **Quality Review** — Senior analysts review and correct junior work (typically 20–30% rework rate)
6. **Report Writing** — Compile findings into risk committee reports with narrative analysis
7. **Stakeholder Review** — Multiple rounds of review/revision before committee submission

## 4. The Quality Problem (Why More Money Doesn't Help)

The manual process has structural quality issues that no amount of headcount can fix:

| Issue | Impact | Source |
|-------|--------|--------|
| **Sample-based coverage** | Analysts can only read ~20% of incoming documents. 80% go unreviewed. | Industry observation — KPMG Cost Transformation in Risk (2024) |
| **Inconsistency** | 200 analysts = 200 different interpretations of the same risk taxonomy. | Industry standard — acknowledged in KPMG Global Risk and Compliance Benchmark |
| **Latency** | Reports are typically 6–8 weeks behind events. Risk committees see stale data. | Basel Committee PSMOR guidance (ongoing) |
| **Poor audit trail** | "Why was this categorised as operational risk?" — "Because the analyst thought so." | ECB supervisory guide on risk data aggregation and reporting (2024) |
| **Rework rate** | 20–30% of manual extractions require correction during QA review. | Industry estimate — KPMG |
| **Attrition** | 41% of senior compliance officers retired in 2024–2025 (talent crisis) | Garrett & Fields "2025 Compliance Talent Crisis" |
| **Vacancy duration** | Average 18 months to fill senior compliance roles | Same |
| **Staffing gaps** | 43% of global banks report regulatory work going undone due to staffing | Same |
